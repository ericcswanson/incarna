ADVENTURE PRINCIPAL<\/span>\r\nDo not let strict campaign or challenge level dictate your use of adventures<\/a>. Story and play should be able to be modified to support most measures of Player Character capability. A good Game Master<\/a> can generally use a system & tools to support them in telling a story<\/a> with whatever characters and circumstances the Players<\/a> have.<\/div>\n<\/div>\n

<\/span>Using the Adventure Materials<\/span><\/h3>

<\/span>Familiarize<\/span><\/h4>

Read through the entire adventure and familiarize yourself with the entire scenario and encounter set. If its too much to track, break the game session and milestones into what you can manage - generally it is better to end early with a good experience than fumble through a poor one. Materials are presented here in groupings of the most likely order the players will experience them in. If they go off-script, you must be ready to adjust and improvise.<\/span><\/p>

<\/span>Maximize Online Materials<\/span><\/h4>

The optimal experience for using these materials is as online content. This material contains links to material outside the text - familiarize yourself with this content as well. Follow the links, and print out or have loaded on a device ahead of time all materials needed to effectively run this adventure. Print friendly PDF or ePub versions also maintain the links in most cases. <\/p>

  • All linked materials were publicly available at the time of original publication.<\/li>
  • Linked materials usually contain more information about creatures and places, though some are to assist the Game Master's running of the adventure. <\/li>
  • The stats for creatures are not presented. It is assumed GMs have access to system source books or materials for them. Where possible, creatures are linked to online resources.<\/li><\/ul>\r\n
