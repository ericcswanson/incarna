    <\/span>Premise<\/span><\/h3>\r\n

    The PC's are transported to a place and time nearby in The Dreamscape<\/a>. they are thrust into the role of trying to change the course of events someplace out of time by reliving the battles that happened in a city sacked by an evil marauding army. If they can change enough, it will have a rippling effect in their own world, stopping a great calamity about to happen. This adventure is the largest scenario of the Mad Merchant of Fate<\/a> Adventure. It can be run as part of that, or as an adventure unto itself.\n<\/p>\r\n

    Motivation<\/b>: MacGuffin<\/a>? \n\r\n
    The PC's want to escape the land of the mirror in order to escape the dream they are imprisoned in.\n<\/p>\r\n

    Background Facts<\/b>:

    It all seems familiar, there are only nearly forgotten myths and half-remembered tales from 4000 years ago. <\/p>\r\n

      \r\n
    • An Eldritch Knight fought here, and possibly sacrificed himself to stop the onslaught a mixed army of Orrish (goblin and ork) and evil savage humans. It can be none other than the City of Kwalando-Lai (a strange eastern-sounding name); Sir Bartrem, the Moon-Runner. Sir Bartrem\u2019s sight was said to be eternally vigilant, a light in the darkness of many places which had fallen to darkness. <\/li>\r\n
    • A great horde of the creatures of darkness had caused an eclipse of the sun to remain permanent. In the dim light, the horde began by sacking the lands of this famous Eldritch Knight and those around him and under his protection. His fabled city of Kwalando-Lai was razed. The horde was eventually contained, though by what means is unknown - it simply vanishes from history. Sir Bartrem was never heard of again. <\/li>\r\n
    • It was said Sir Bertram had a magic mirror that he could see and travel into any place with through his dreams, and that it was shattered when his enemies sacked his home, and with that the dreams of his peace for the land. <\/li>\r\n<\/ul>\n\r\n<\/div>\r\n

      <\/span>Getting there<\/span><\/h3>\r\n

      Assembling the Party<\/b>: As you drift off to sleep, your thoughts are troubled. Your dreams are strange, almost as if you are dreaming in your own dreams. Night after night you have had disturbed sleep, and rest has been hard to come by\u2026 war has disturbed your sleep night after night. Now you dream of a place where water is plentiful, crops grow every year, and few want for anything. In your dreams is a face - a face unrecognizable. It is a man\u2019s and he watches over the people of his land, dreaming his own dreams of peace and prosperity, and genuinely working to make the dreams happen in his waking hours. You see this take place across many cities, towns and villages - always the same face, seemingly on multiple worlds even. <\/p>\r\n

      When you wake, it is to the sound of snorting horses, horns in the distance and the smell of smoke. You cast about for the source and realize it's just beyond the door to your room. Your companions seem nowhere to be found, so you hastility dress in your armor, gathering weapons and equipment to do battle. You burst through the door hoping to take the attackers by surprise and stumble into a smoke shrouded corridor. Quickly you lost track of where you are, and stumble blindly. The smell in the air changes from smoke and sweat, to a field and wet grass. You shake off the cobwebs from your senses and look around. You as well as others stand around fully equipped. You get the sense that all of them had the same experience as you - no one is sure how to react. Helmets are doffed, hands raised, and faces shown. <\/p>\n<\/div>\n

      Allow the party to make greetings and introductions to each other. Taking stock of good is what they would normally be carrying while exploring or investigating. There are no mounts or servants, only a day worth of food and drink. <\/p>\n

      After a while, their peace will be interrupted<\/b>:<\/p>\n

      In the distance you hear multiple horns – some sort of military code is being blown. You run to the nearest rise to the north and drop down along its top, spreading out. To the north is a city of several thousand inhabitants.The city is obviously the site of a siege – many small first are still lit and dwindling. You see soldiers running into the city and eventually the march of feet in unison. Within an hour, you can tell they have left on the far side of the town. It appears sacked and abandoned.\n<\/p>\n<\/div>\n

Rising above the wreckage, there is a large tower at the center, scorched and ruined but still standing. An army is clearly leaving on the far side, going away from you. <\/p>\n

      <\/i> Worg: AC 13 | HP 30 > bite

      <\/span>City up Close<\/span><\/h3>\n

      The streets are littered with barricades and bodies. The city held several thousand – and had at least 3 major roads running through it – obviously a major intersection for trade. Nothing seems to move but a few rats, dogs, and flies – it appears as if all the inhabitants have been killed.<\/p>\n

      Have the PC\u2019s make 3 Perception checks as they move through; Nothing truly matters, just keeping the party on their toes. Once the party has had the situation fully sink in, you can remind the PC\u2019s that by all appearances, the army has left and that it would be safe to scout. <\/p>\n

      <\/span>Scouting for a Route Into Kwalando-Lai<\/span><\/h4>\n

      From your vantage point you can see there is a fairly open route from what you think is the east side of the town, from the edge of town to the tower\u2019s remains. There are still a lot of ruins and broken barricades, but the east west route runs directly through the grounds of the tower, and the east route is mostly clear. It is clear that from the vantage point of the fortified tower, the entire city could be seen, and the hunt for survivors would be that much more effective. <\/p>\n

      There is little else to be seen in the sacked city. Everywhere the PC\u2019s go is devastation. If the PC\u2019s need to be nudged, you can have them follow a figure that they see scampering through the ruins. This figure will lead them on a chase, eventually leading down the eastern road.\n<\/p>\n

      A Figure in the Ruins…<\/b><\/p>\n

      The road is full of dead bodies and broken barricades. The constant sound of flies and knowledge of what they are here for makes you sick. There were no exception, no one was spared or given mercy. You press your way through the wreckage, keeping the tower in sight the whole time. The eastern road seems to lead right up to it – the only road still passable without having to move much debris. <\/p>\n

      <\/span>What actually Happens in Combat Re-enactment<\/span><\/h3>\n
      The PCs take the place of those that were physically there. Their actions are reflected in the ideas and actions of the beings they “inhabit”. While those beings don’t have the same power, the results of the PCs combat are reflected in ambushes, ideas for attack, and the combat of the masses. Its confusing as the dreamscape<\/a> “translates” the PC\u2019s action into results in the siege of the city, but there is a soothing voice that speaks to the PC\u2019s, speaking of dreams, fate, and the actions of the one and the actions of the many. <\/div>\n

      <\/span>The Tower of Sir Bartrem<\/span><\/h2>\n

      Outside, the tower appears to still be structurally sound, soaring what must be nearly 70 feet in the air and having a diameter of nearly 40 feet. Several battering implements are broken and scattered around its base. Smoke wafts out the few broken broken windows (of which there are none on the other levels) at the top, but no flames lick up its sides. A scorched mound of bodies is piled several feet away – the smell of roasting flesh is strong on the breeze. The sign of a large moon is chiseled into the arch above the broken door. <\/p>\n

      The bodies are all the soldiers who died in the attack – there are 2 dozen or more dead (and who knows how many wounded limped away from the carnage)! The tabard of the black hand upon the golden sun with a sickly blue halo can be made out between the burns and blood. The tower is 5 stories of 70 feet tall with a broken roof. The doors are battered down and you can easily peer inside.<\/p>\n
